# Benchmarks by Industry

Order-of-magnitude CPA / CTR / CVR benchmarks. Use as sanity-check for "expected CPA range" in channel plans, **not** as targets. Real targets come from your own LTV math; benchmarks just tell you whether your strategy doc's number is plausible.

## Caveats

- Benchmarks vary 3–5x by sub-vertical, geography, season, and ad quality
- "Industry standard" is not a useful goal; beat *your own* prior period
- These are 2024–2025 ranges from public reports (WordStream, Databox, AdEspresso) plus aggregated practitioner reports — verify with current data when shaping a strategy

## Google Search

| Industry | Avg CPC | CTR | CVR | CPA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| B2B SaaS | $4–10 | 3–5% | 3–5% | $80–250 |
| B2C SaaS | $2–6 | 4–7% | 5–10% | $25–100 |
| E-commerce | $1–3 | 5–8% | 2–4% | $20–80 |
| Legal / Finance | $20–80 | 2–4% | 3–6% | $300–1,000 |
| Healthcare | $5–15 | 3–5% | 3–7% | $80–300 |
| Education | $3–8 | 3–6% | 4–8% | $50–200 |
| Local services | $2–5 | 4–6% | 5–10% | $30–100 |

**Branded Search** is always 2–4x cheaper and 5–10x higher CVR — bench against itself.

## Meta (Facebook + Instagram)

| Industry | Avg CPM | CTR (link) | CVR (LP) | CPA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| B2B SaaS | $20–40 | 1–2% | 3–5% | $60–250 |
| B2C SaaS | $10–25 | 1.5–3% | 4–8% | $20–80 |
| E-commerce | $10–20 | 1.5–3% | 1.5–3% | $25–80 |
| Healthcare | $25–50 | 1–2% | 3–5% | $80–300 |
| Education | $15–30 | 1.5–3% | 3–6% | $40–150 |

**Retargeting** typically 2–3x cheaper CPA than prospecting on Meta.

## LinkedIn

| Type | Avg CPC | CPM | CTR | CPA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sponsored Content (SI) | $6–15 | $30–60 | 0.4–0.8% | $200–800 |
| Sponsored Content (Video) | $6–15 | $40–80 | 0.5–1.0% | $200–800 |
| Document Ads | $6–15 | $30–60 | 0.6–1.2% | $150–600 |
| Message Ads | n/a | n/a | 30–50% open | $40–200 |
| Conversation Ads | n/a | n/a | 35–55% open | $50–300 |

LinkedIn is 3–5x more expensive than Meta on CPM but converts senior B2B audiences at much higher rates. Don't compare CPA cross-platform without weighting deal size.

## TikTok

| Industry | CPM | CTR | CVR | CPA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| E-commerce | $5–12 | 1–2% | 2–4% | $20–60 |
| B2C SaaS / apps | $6–15 | 1–3% | 3–6% | $25–80 |
| B2B (rare) | $10–25 | 0.8–1.5% | 1–3% | $80–250 |

TikTok rewards native vertical video; static/repurposed Meta creative tanks performance.

## Reddit

- CPC: $0.50–$3.00
- Conversation Ads (interactive): higher engagement, similar CPA range
- Tight audience match (right subreddit) → lower CPA
- Generic targeting → high impressions, weak conversion

## Display / GDN

CPC $0.50–$2.00 but CVR typically 0.5–1% on prospecting → high CPA. Display works for retargeting and brand reach, rarely for direct response. **Default out-of-scope** in Office of CMO unless retargeting.

## How to use

When `/digital` sets an "Expected CPA range" for a channel, the order of preference is:
- Your own historical data if you have it
- These benchmarks otherwise
- A note: "Cite source. We expect $X–$Y on Meta based on prior similar segments + benchmarks; will reconcile after first 14 days."

Then during iteration, `/digital` classifies CAC as inside/outside expected range. If a channel runs 3x outside the expected range, the strategy thesis was wrong, not the execution.
