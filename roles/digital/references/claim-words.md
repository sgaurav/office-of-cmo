# Claim Words Requiring Substantiation

Words that trigger automatic "where's the evidence?" questions. If a creative uses one, the Compliance Reviewer demands a citable source before PASS.

## Superlatives (require third-party verification)
- "best", "#1", "leading", "top-rated", "world's only"
- "fastest", "cheapest", "smartest"
- "industry-standard"

→ Either back with a third-party study with citation, OR rephrase ("loved by 1,200+", "trusted by [specific list]").

## Guarantees (require real guarantee mechanism)
- "guaranteed", "100% guaranteed", "or your money back"
- "risk-free", "no risk"

→ Must point to actual refund/SLA policy linked from the LP.

## Performance numbers (require methodology)
- "10x faster", "5x ROI", "save 47 minutes"
- "increase X by N%"
- "reduce Y by N%"

→ Must cite source: internal data with sample size, customer case study, third-party benchmark. Methodology must be linkable.

## Earnings / income claims (FTC heavy)
- "make $X/month", "earn $Y", "double your revenue"
- "passive income", "financial freedom"

→ Must include "Results not typical" + methodology link. FTC actively litigates these. In doubt, rephrase or remove.

## Health / medical claims
- "cure", "treat", "prevent", "diagnose"
- "doctor-recommended" without specifics
- "FDA-approved" (must literally be approved)
- "clinically proven"

→ Healthcare advertisers are restricted on most platforms; specific certifications required.

## Comparison claims (legally fraught)
- "Better than [Competitor]"
- "Unlike [Competitor]"
- "[Competitor] does X. We do Y."

→ Comparison advertising is legal but must be truthful and substantiated. Use specific feature comparisons, not general superiority. Include a "compared to <competitor> as of <date>" footnote.

## Time-bound urgency (often fake)
- "Limited time"
- "Last chance"
- "Today only"
- "Sale ends [date]"

→ Must be true. Repeat use of "limited time" without an actual end date is FTC bait-and-switch.

## Endorsements / testimonials
- Customer quotes — require:
  - Real customer (named or pseudonymous with disclosure)
  - Permission on file (DocuSign or email)
  - "Results not typical" if outlier
  - Compensation disclosure if compensated

## Influencer / creator claims
- Must include `#ad` / `#sponsored` per FTC
- Apply on TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, X — even when platform UI doesn't enforce

## Anti-pattern phrases (lazy claims, rephrase)
- "Industry-leading"
- "World-class"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Game-changing"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Synergy"

These are not substantiable claims; they are filler. Rephrase to specifics or cut.

## Platform-specific watch words
- **Meta**: "you" / "your" + sensitive attribute ("Are you tired of being a struggling parent?") — personal attribute violation
- **Meta**: "weight loss", "before/after" — automatic disapproval for most accounts
- **Google**: "free trial" + sensitive category (gambling/finance) — extra review
- **LinkedIn**: "Are you a [protected attribute]?" — targeting policy violation
