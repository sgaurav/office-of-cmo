# Copy Frameworks

The hook stack `/ads-creative-generate` riffs across. Pick the right framework per hook; never run all 8 hooks in the same framework.

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solve
1. State the problem in the audience's language
2. Agitate (twist the knife — why it's worse than they admit)
3. Solve (your product, in one line)

Example: "Your CSV exports take 40 minutes. The team waits. The CFO asks why. <Tool> exports in 8 seconds."

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge
1. Before: their world today
2. After: their world after the product
3. Bridge: how your product gets them there

Example: "Before: 3 hours of triage every Monday. After: triage runs itself overnight. Bridge: <tool>'s rules engine."

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
1. Attention: bold opener
2. Interest: relevant detail
3. Desire: outcome they want
4. Action: clear CTA

## Hook-Story-Offer (Russell Brunson)
1. Hook: stop-scrolling line
2. Story: 2–3 sentence narrative ("I used to <X>...")
3. Offer: specific, time-bound, low-friction next step

## Status flip
"Stop being the team that <undesirable status>. Start being the team that <desirable status>."

## Contrarian
"Everyone says <conventional wisdom>. We disagree because <reason>."

Works only when the contrarian take is genuinely defensible.

## Stat-led
"<Specific number> of <segment> <do undesirable thing>. Here's why that's wrong."

Pull numbers from your own data or cite sources. Made-up stats kill trust.

## Question-led
"What if <pain> just... didn't happen?"

Best for outcome-led angles. Avoid rhetorical questions that feel pandering.

## Direct benefit (no framework, just the truth)
"<Outcome>, in <timeframe>, for <audience>." e.g., "Closed books in 3 days, not 3 weeks. For controllers at $10–50M ARR companies."

## Anti-patterns

- Adjective stacks: "powerful, intuitive, scalable AI-powered solution"
- "Revolutionize / transform / unlock" — these words have no signal
- Hooks that try to do all the work in one sentence: "Save 10 hours a week with our AI-powered automation tool that helps your team collaborate" — three messages, zero punch
- Curiosity gap without a benefit: "You won't believe what we did" — bait

## Voice modifiers (apply after framework)

- **Direct/dry-witty:** trim every adjective; let the sentence end early
- **Technical:** include the specific noun/term the audience uses internally
- **Warm/founder:** "We" / "I" / "we did this because…"
- **Punchy:** ≤8 words preferred for hooks
