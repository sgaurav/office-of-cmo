# Platform Character Limits

Hard limits and "optimal" zones (where copy doesn't get truncated by feed UI). Always design to optimal, not max.

## Meta (Facebook + Instagram)

### Feed (image / video)
- Primary text: 125 char before "see more" cut on most placements; 2,200 char hard max
- Headline: 27 char optimal (mobile feed); 40 char hard
- Description: 27 char optimal; 30 char hard
- Link description: 27 char optimal
- CTA: button — choose from preset list (`Sign Up`, `Learn More`, `Get Demo`, `Shop Now`, etc.)

### Stories / Reels (9:16)
- Caption: 125 char optimal
- Headline overlay: keep ≤8 words for sound-off readability

### Carousel
- Card headline: 27 char optimal per card
- Up to 10 cards; usually 3–5 best

## Google Ads

### Responsive Search Ads (RSA)
- 15 headlines, 30 char each (hard)
- 4 descriptions, 90 char each (hard)
- 4 sitelinks, 25 char each
- 4 callouts, 25 char each
- Display path: 15 char × 2

### Performance Max
- 5 headlines, 30 char
- 5 long headlines, 90 char
- 5 descriptions, 90 char
- Same image specs as Display

### Display
- Square headline: 30 char
- Long headline: 90 char
- Description: 90 char

### YouTube In-Stream
- Headline: 15 char
- Description: 90 char
- 6-second bumper has no copy beyond visual

## LinkedIn

### Sponsored Content (Single Image / Video)
- Intro text: 150 char optimal (truncates on mobile feed); 600 char hard
- Headline: 70 char optimal; 200 char hard
- Description: 200 char optimal (only shows on some surfaces)
- CTA: preset (`Apply`, `Download`, `Learn More`, `Sign Up`, etc.)

### Document Ads
- Intro text: 150 char optimal
- 8-page max (PDF), but 4–5 pages converts better

### Message / Conversation Ads
- Subject: 60 char
- Greeting + message body: ≤500 char each
- 5 CTAs max

## TikTok

- Caption: 100 char optimal; 2,200 hard
- Hook overlay: ≤6 words for thumb-stopping clarity
- Sound-on by default (use it; don't burn captions for UX, do for accessibility)

## X (Twitter)

- Promoted Tweet: 280 char (same as organic)
- Take Over: image with overlay ≤6 words

## Reddit

- Title: 300 char hard, but 50–100 optimal
- Body: 10,000 char (use sparingly; conversational > pitch)

## Universal rules

- Numbers/specifics beat round numbers ("save 47 minutes" > "save time")
- Lead with the outcome, not the feature
- Avoid emoji unless the brand voice explicitly endorses them
- Never use ALL CAPS for full sentences (Meta and Google reject it)
- Never use exclamation point stacks ("!!!")
